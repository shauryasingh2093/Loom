The Idea Behind Loom

The core idea I had was to move away from the typical way apps show wellness data. Most apps today show things like charts, dashboards, numbers, or metrics, which can feel overwhelming and technical.

Instead, I wanted the interface to feel more natural, intuitive, and human, where users can understand their state visually without needing to interpret data.

Why the Name Loom

I thought about renaming the system from Resonance to Loom.

A loom is a tool used to weave threads together, and that metaphor fits perfectly with the idea that our mind, body, and environment are constantly interacting and shaping how we feel.

So instead of treating these signals as separate metrics, the app treats them like threads in a fabric that are constantly weaving together.

Core Concept

In Loom, the user’s state is represented as a woven structure made of threads.

Each type of thread represents a different signal:

• Environment threads – signals from surroundings like noise, light, and crowd density
• Body threads – signals like muscle tension or stress
• Mind threads – signals like focus, rumination, or mental drift

These threads interact with each other to form the user’s current weave.

How Stress Appears

When everything is balanced:

The threads are smooth and evenly woven.

When the user becomes stressed or overstimulated:

The threads begin to stretch, vibrate, or tangle.

This allows users to visually see how their state is changing, instead of reading numbers or graphs.

Role of the AI Companion

The app includes an AI companion that helps guide the user when the system detects imbalance.

For example:

If the user appears stressed, the AI might suggest:

• taking a short break
• stepping outside
• moving to a quieter place
• doing a quick reset activity

Untangling as the Core Interaction

The main interaction idea is untangling the threads.

When the user completes a suggested action:

The tangled threads begin to loosen and untangle slightly.

As the user continues to follow helpful actions:

The weave becomes smoother and more balanced.

This creates a visual feeling of progress and recovery, where the user can literally see their state improving.

Why This Approach Is Different

Instead of showing data dashboards, Loom shows relationships between signals.

Users don’t have to interpret charts or numbers.
They simply observe the behavior of the threads.

This makes the experience feel:

• intuitive
• calming
• playful
• more human-centered

Overall Vision

The goal of Loom is to create a system where people can perceive the invisible connections between their mind, body, and environment, and gradually restore balance by untangling their weave.

Rather than tracking data, the user is interacting with their own state in a visual and intuitive way.
